Course outline

Week 1, Jan 6 – 10

  • Lecture 1: Statistics review
    • Reading: Can a biologist fix a radio?
    • Listening: Linear Digressions: The Normal Distribution and the Central Limit Theorem
  • Lecture 2: Fitting & Regression
    • Listening: Linear Digressions: The assumptions of ordinary least squares
    • Reading: Points of Significance: Simple linear regression
    • Listening: Linear Digressions: Convex (and non-convex) optimization
  • Lab: Programming and Git Primer, due 1/15 at noon.

Week 2, Jan 13 – 17

  • Reading: Points of Significance: Regression Diagnostics
  • Lecture 3: Fitting & Regression Redux, Regularization
  • Lecture 4: Does my model work? Crossvalidation, bootstrap, and friends
  • Lab: Implementation of Shaffer et al., due 1/23 at 11:59 pm (notebook & submission link)

Week 3, Jan 20 – 24

  • Lecture 5: Bayesian vs. frequentist approaches
    • Linear Digressions: Beware of simple metrics
  • Lecture 6: Reproducible computational workflows
  • Lab: Implementation of Stone et al., due 1/30 at 11:59 pm (notebook & submission link)

Week 4, Jan 27 – Jan 31

  • Reading: Points of Significance: Principal Components Analysis
  • Lecture 7: Dimentionality reduction - PCA and NMF
  • Lecture 8: Partial Least Squares Regression
  • Lab: Implementation of Cosgrove et al., due 2/6 at 11:59 pm (notebook & submission link)

Week 5, Feb 3 – 7

  • Reading: What is a hidden Markov model?
  • Reading: Markov models—Markov chains
  • Reading: Markov models — hidden Markov models
  • Listening: Linear Digressions - Hidden Markov Models (part 2)
  • Lecture 9: Dynamical models
  • Lecture 10: Hidden Markov Models (Example Notebook)
  • Lab: Midterm review, finish selecting project (setup project repository)

Week 6, Feb 10 – 14

  • Final project proposals due 2/11 at 8 pm
  • Lecture 11: Midterm review (previous midterms: W18, W19)
  • Lecture 12: Midterm exam
  • Lab: Implementation of Perelson et al., due 2/20 at 11:59 pm (notebook & submission link)

Week 7, Feb 17 – 21

  • Lecture 13: Clustering (Example Notebook)
  • Lecture 14: Autodiff and Clustering Cont’d (Example Notebook)
  • Lab: Implementation of HMMs for heart rate monitor wearable, due 2/27 at 11:59 pm (notebook & subission link)

Week 8, Feb 24 – Feb 28

  • Reading: Points of Significance: Classification and regression trees
  • Lecture 15: Decision trees
  • Lecture 16: Support vector machines (Example Notebook)
    • Listening: Linear Digressions: Maximal Margin Classifiers
    • Listening: Linear Digressions: The Kernel Trick and Support Vector Machines

Week 9, Mar 2 – 6

  • Lab: Implementation of Masaeli et al., due 3/12 at 11:59 pm (notebook & submission link)

Week 10, Mar 9 – 13

  • Lecture: Project presentations
  • Lab: Further project implementation
  • Final Project, due 3/18 at noon (submit through project Github repository)
